# 📊 Data-Driven Customer Retention & Operations Strategy Case Study

## 📌 1. Project Objective & Business Context
The objective of this project was to act as a strategic advisor for Olist, a major Brazilian e-commerce platform. By evaluating a complex 5-table relational database containing **100,000+ localized transactional records**, I engineered an analytical pipeline to segment the platform's consumer base, diagnose emerging revenue churn, and pinpoint operational supply chain bottlenecks.

## 💡 3. Strategic Insights (The Executive Translation)

### 🚨 The Operational Revenue Leak
The data isolates a critical operational risk: the high-value **"At-Risk Churner" segment contributes a massive 25% of total platform revenue**, yet they are actively abandoning the brand. 
* **The Root Cause:** This segment is experiencing extreme fulfillment friction, averaging **6.4 days of delivery delays**. 
* **The Correlation:** This mid-mile logistics bottleneck directly correlates with customer satisfaction scores plummeting to a critical **3.2 stars**, proving that late shipping—not pricing or product selection—is driving premium user churn.

### 📍 Regional & Portfolio Vulnerabilities
* **Geographic Bottlenecks:** A regional breakdown reveals that localized infrastructure constraints in specific Brazilian states are skewing the platform's baseline fulfillment timelines.
* **Portfolio Exposure:** High-revenue product verticals are heavily exposed to low customer sentiment, representing millions in at-risk revenue due to poor supplier quality control.

---

## 🚀 4. Actionable C-Suite Recommendations

1. **Enforce Logistic Service Level Agreements (SLAs):** Proactively audit and renegotiate contracts with regional fulfillment partners in high-delay states to compress the 6.4-day bottleneck down to an operational standard of < 2.0 days.
2. **Deploy Targeted Capital Reallocation:** Shift 15% of underperforming marketing expenditures away from the low-value/occasional buyer segment. Reallocate these funds to launch a proactive, high-margin customer service "win-back" voucher campaign explicitly targeting the high-value "At-Risk" tier before permanent brand attrition occurs.
